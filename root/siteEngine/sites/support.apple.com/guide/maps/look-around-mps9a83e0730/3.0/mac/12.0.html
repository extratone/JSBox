Look around in Maps on Mac

With the larger screen on your Mac, you can look around and see what’s ahead. For some locations, you can even see an interactive 3D view.

Open Maps for me

  1. In the Maps app on your Mac, click in the search field, then enter an address or other information, such as an intersection, landmark, or business.

  2. Do one of the following, if available:

    • Click Look Around below a location in the search results.

    • Select a location, then click the Look Around button in the toolbar.

    • Select a location. In the place card, click the image with the Look Around button in the lower-left corner.

  3. In the image, you can do any of the following:

    • Pan: Drag the image left or right.

    • Move forward: Click in front of you in the look around view.

    • Zoom in or out: On a trackpad, pinch two fingers open or closed.

    • View another point of interest: Click another location on the map.

    • Enter full screen: Click the Enter Full Screen button . To exit full screen, click the Exit Full Screen button .

  4. When you’re done, click the Close button .
