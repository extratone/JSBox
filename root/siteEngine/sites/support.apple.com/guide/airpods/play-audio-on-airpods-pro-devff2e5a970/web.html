Play audio on AirPods Pro

When you’re wearing your AirPods Pro, you can use the Force Sensor to play, pause, or skip audio playing on your iPhone, iPad, iPod touch, Apple Watch, Mac, or Apple TV.

Play and pause audio

  1. Wear one or both AirPods, and make sure they’re connected to your device.

  2. Open an audio app, then play something. For example, you can play a song in Apple Music, or listen to an audio book in Books.

    To pause audio while listening with both AirPods, remove one. When you return it to your ear, playback resumes automatically.

    Note: You can also press the Force Sensor to pause audio. Press the Force Sensor again to resume playback.

Stop the audio

Remove both AirPods Pro from your ears, or if you’re wearing just one, remove it.

Skip tracks

You can double-press the Force Sensor on either of your AirPods Pro to skip to another track in many audio apps. For example, you can double-press the Force Sensor to skip to the next song in Apple Music, or to skip to the previous episode in Podcasts.

To skip to the previous song, triple-press the Force Sensor.

Protect your hearing

You can monitor and limit the volume of your AirPods using your iPhone, iPad, or iPod touch. See Check your headphone levels in the iPhone User Guide.

This guide is for iOS 15.1, iPadOS 15.1, watchOS 8.1, tvOS 15.1, macOS Monterey, or later.
